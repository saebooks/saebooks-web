ECPI & actuarial certificate · ART SMSF · FY26

Income from assets supporting a retirement-phase income stream is exempt from the 15% accumulation-phase tax (ITAA 1997 s.295-385 to s.295-400). Where the fund is unsegregated (or partially), an actuarial certificate must apportion the exempt slice. With the Disregarded Small Fund Assets rule under s.295-385(7) — triggered when any member has a Total Super Balance > A$1.6m on the prior 30-Jun and receives a retirement-phase pension at any point in the year — the fund must use the proportionate method even where it would otherwise be segregated. SAE Books wires the ECPI calc into the GL, drives the TBAR feed, and produces an actuary-ready data pack.

ACT 02

Disregarded Small Fund Assets test · s.295-385(7)

  1. 1 — Is the fund an SMSF (or small APRA fund) for FY26? Yes — SMSF (SIS Act 1993 s.17A).
  2. 2 — Does the fund have a member who, on prior 30-Jun (2025-06-30), had a Total Super Balance > A$1.6m? Yes — R Sauer TSB A$1,684,260 (across all funds: ART SMSF A$1.62m + employer fund A$64,260).
  3. 3 — Does that member receive a retirement-phase income stream at any point during FY26 from any super interest? Yes — R Sauer’s TRIS in ART SMSF was in retirement phase from 2024-07-01 (member turned 65, automatic conversion under ITAA 1997 s.307-80(4)).
  4. 4 — Both yes ⇒ DSFA applies for FY26. Fund cannot use the segregated current pension assets method (s.295-385) even where the trust deed segregates and even where 100% of assets support pensions. Must use the proportionate method under s.295-390 for the whole year. DSFA triggered

DSFA was inserted by Treasury Laws Amendment (2019 Measures No. 3) Act 2020 with effect FY18 onwards. ATO interpretive: LCR 2021/2. The fund retains the ECPI exemption — only the method of calculating it changes — but the actuarial certificate is now mandatory and the segregation election is overridden.

ACT 07

Market read · SMSF software vs SAE Books

SMSF-specific tools
  • BGL Simple Fund 360 — market leader. End-to-end SMSF ledger with daily valuations, DSFA test, TBAR generator, integrated actuary export (Accurium, Lime, Heffron). Excellent for SMSF-only practices.
  • Class — cloud-native competitor; similar feature set; strong reporting.
  • Supermate — legacy; declining.
  • Xero / MYOB / Reckon — not SMSF-aware. Trust-account ledgers but no DSFA, no TBAR, no actuary export. Tax-agent treats the SMSF separately in BGL/Class.
SAE Books · cross-entity
  • The SMSF lives in the same party-graph as the operating company, the trust, and the directors — Div 7A loans, in-house assets, related-unit-trust investments, and NALI risk all surface as one register.
  • DSFA test runs on the unified TSB (across all super interests for each member), not just this fund’s balance.
  • Div 296 shadow ledger (A$3m threshold, FY26 proposed) projects member-level liability across calendar years.
  • Actuary data pack exports in Accurium/Lime/Heffron schemas — nothing displaces the actuary itself, but the data hand-off is a one-click GL-sourced JSON instead of an Excel re-key.
  • For SMSF-only practices, SAE Books does not displace BGL Simple Fund 360 or Class — but for families with operating-business + trust + SMSF the cross-entity coherence pays.
