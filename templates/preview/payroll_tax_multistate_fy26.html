Multi-state payroll tax · FY26 · 5 jurisdictions

Payroll tax is harmonised in word but not in arithmetic. Each state sets its own threshold, rate, surcharge, and contractor exemption schedule. SAE Group has wages in NSW, VIC, QLD, SA, WA for FY26 — A$8.42m total Australian-wages base, three monthly returns and two quarterly returns running in parallel, one annual reconciliation per jurisdiction by 21 July 2026. The nexus engine (Payroll Tax Act 2007 s.11 harmonised) routes each pay-event line to the correct jurisdiction; grouping under s.71 collapses related entities into one threshold per state.

ACT 03

Nexus cascade · s.11 harmonised · which state taxes which dollar

5-step nexus cascade per pay event
  1. 1 — Services wholly in one AU jurisdiction in the month? Tax there. s.11(1)(a)
  2. 2 — Services in >1 AU jurisdiction in the month, employee’s principal place of residence is in an AU jurisdiction? Tax there. s.11(2)(a)
  3. 3 — Otherwise, employer’s ABN address in AU? Tax there. s.11(2)(b)
  4. 4 — Otherwise, wages paid in AU jurisdiction? Tax where paid. s.11(2)(c)
  5. 5 — Otherwise, services wholly outside AU for ≤6 mth? Tax at employee’s last AU residence. > 6 mth = exempt. s.11(3) · s.11(4)

9 exemptions · s.32(2) harmonised
  1. (a) services involving supply of both materials and labour, materials substantial
  2. (b) services performed by contractor ≤ 90 days in financial year for principal
  3. (c) services where labour is ancillary to the supply of goods/equipment
  4. (d) contractor ordinarily provides services to public generally
  5. (e) services performed by contractor incidental to other services
  6. (f) Commissioner-approved exemption
  7. (g) contractor provides services for ≤ 180 days and the Commissioner is satisfied
  8. (h) services performed by contractor who has employed others to perform them
  9. (i) contractor engages two or more own employees who perform the services

If any of (a)–(i) is satisfied, the contractor payment is excluded from deemed wages. If none, the labour component (or 90% if no split provided per Rulings PTA014/PTA018) is taxable.

ACT 07

Market read · where multi-state payroll tax lives elsewhere

Xero / MYOB / Reckon
  • Xero — payroll-tax module produces a per-state summary report only after the user flags each employee’s state in the employee record. No nexus engine, no contractor s.32 cascade, no MHL/MH&WB calc, no apprentice rebate, no postcode regional flag. The annual recon is a CSV export the user re-keys into the OSR portal.
  • MYOB AE/AO + MYOB Greentree — better contractor and grouping handling, but the practice still maintains a separate Excel master for s.32 exemption evidence and a manual MHL/MH&WB sidecar.
  • Reckon Accounts / One — payroll tax handled as a periodic JE; the recon is essentially done by hand against pay-runs filtered by state.
  • BGL Simple Fund / CCH iFirm — practice-side compliance modules, not on the client’s books.
SAE Books · native
  • Per-pay-event nexus engine resolves s.11 cascade against PPR, work location, ABN address, paid-from bank account — no per-employee state flag, no Excel master.
  • Designated Group Employer (DGE) lives in the entity-graph; s.71 grouping pulls related corps and employees-in-common automatically and surfaces threshold-allocation arithmetic on the workpaper.
  • Contractor 9-exemption matrix runs as the contractor is paid; if no exemption holds, the labour component lands in deemed wages with PTA014/PTA018 90% default.
  • Mental Health Levy (QLD) and MH&WB Surcharge (VIC) bracket-aware; postcode regional flag for VIC; apprentice rebate (QLD) and apprentice exemption (VIC) auto-applied.
  • 5-state lodgement pack at 30 June: per-OSR XML / JSON exports plus a reconciliation memo against the monthly returns, ready for the practice to review.
