Private-company loan, payment and forgiveness register under ITAA 1936 Pt III Div 7A. Catches any value moving from SAE Pty Ltd or Saueesti Pty Ltd to a shareholder or associate that isn't a complying loan, salary, or franked dividend. Lodgement deadline runs in parallel with the company tax return — must convert loans to complying terms before the lodgement day for the year of advance (s.109N). Minimum Yearly Repayment · s.109E formula

Statutory formula s.109E(6)
MYR =     amount of loan not repaid by end of previous year × benchmark rate
       ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
         1 − (1 + benchmark rate) ^ −(remaining term in years)
Equivalent to a level-amortising annuity. Indexed annually to RBA SVR for housing on the last business day of May preceding the income year.

Why the cap matters

Deemed dividends under s.109D/E/T are limited to the company's distributable surplus for the income year (s.109Y). Even a non-complying advance > surplus is capped at the surplus. If the surplus is A$0, the deemed dividend is nil (e.g. Carey v FCT [2009] AATA 116).

SAE Books surfaces the surplus calc on the GL so advisors can pre-clear cash extractions against the cap before lodgement. Xero and MYOB stop at "balance of intercompany loan account" — they do not compute s.109Y.
