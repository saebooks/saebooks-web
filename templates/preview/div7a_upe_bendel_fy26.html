Bendel landed at the High Court — A$884,260 of historic UPEs back outside Div 7A.

On 14 March 2026 the High Court dismissed the Commissioner's appeal in FCT v Bendel [2026] HCA 9, affirming the Full Federal Court (Bendel v FCT [2025] FCAFC 15). A subsisting Unpaid Present Entitlement of a beneficiary corporate is not a "loan" within ITAA 1936 s.109D(3). ATO withdrew TD 2022/11 on 3 April 2026; the historic TR 2010/3 position is dead. Saueesti Trust's 6 historic UPEs to Sauerland Pty Ltd (A$884,260 cumulative) revert to non-Div 7A treatment. PCG 2017/13 sub-trust arrangements collapse back into the head trust; minimum yearly repayments cancelled for FY26 onward.

All three covered in Fire 52 · Div 7A loan compliance. Bendel does not affect actual loans — only the UPE-as-loan deeming. The s.109D(3)(b) "in substance loan" reach is unchanged.
If UPE is later "converted" to a loan
A subsisting UPE remains outside Div 7A indefinitely while it remains a UPE — that is the Bendel ratio. But if at any time the trustee and the beneficiary corporate document an intention to convert the UPE into a loan (e.g. via offset, novation, deed of acknowledgement), Div 7A engages from that moment — not retrospectively.
Saueesti deed clause 14.7 specifically preserves the UPE without conversion. Trustee minute 2026-05-04 confirms intent to leave the entitlement unpaid; no novation contemplated.

s.100A interaction — Guardian / Bendel together

Bendel removed Div 7A pressure but s.100A still tests reimbursement

Bendel does not wash through to s.100A. Unpaid present entitlements that produce a non-arm's-length benefit to someone other than the beneficiary remain susceptible to s.100A reimbursement-agreement attack regardless of the loan analysis. FCT v Guardian AIT [2023] FCAFC 3 confirmed that "ordinary family or commercial dealing" excludes long-term unpaid distributions where money is being deployed for the trustee or another beneficiary's benefit.

Saueesti's position remains green-zone under PCG 2022/2 §22-44 on these grounds:

  • UPEs are accounted as a separate working-capital advance in the books of Sauerland with restricted access
  • No funds have been deployed for the personal benefit of trust beneficiaries other than Sauerland (the corp beneficiary itself)
  • Trust resolutions and beneficiary returns align (Fire 55 + Fire 61 cross-match)
  • UPE balance reduces each year via payment of franked dividends from Sauerland → Saueesti circulation (last 4 yrs A$280k cycled)
